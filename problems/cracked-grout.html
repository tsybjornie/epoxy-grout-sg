The Problem

Why This Happens

Cement grout becomes brittle over time, especially in areas with temperature changes (near ovens, windows) or building movement. Once cracked, water enters and causes tile loosening, mold, and structural damage.

FAQ

Is cracked grout a serious problem?

Yes! Cracked grout allows water to seep behind tiles, causing hidden mold, tile loosening, and potential water damage to walls and floors. Fix it early before it becomes expensive.

Can you fix just the cracked areas or do you need to redo everything?

We can do spot repairs, but we recommend full regrouting for best results. Mixing old cement grout with new epoxy creates an uneven look. Full regrouting gives you a uniform, waterproof finish.

My grout is crumbling in the shower. Is this normal?

It is common but not normal. Shower grout deteriorates faster due to constant water exposure. Epoxy grout is specifically designed for wet areas - it will not crumble or deteriorate.
